Imagine a world of mighty legions, colossal architecture, and emperors ruling vast lands: that was the Roman Empire. From humble beginnings, Rome conquered and civilized much of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, leaving behind enduring legacies in law, language, and engineering. But internal strife and external pressures eventually led to its dramatic decline, proving that even the greatest empires crumble.
